# Ownership: Don't Let Your Boss Do Your Job

## Key Takeaways

- Silence reads as inaction — the org sees absence of communication, not your good intentions; **credibility erodes regardless of eventual resolution quality**
- When your boss picks up your dropped problem, they're not helping you — they're **doing your job**, and everyone sees it
- Ownership = **Signal + Route + Verify**. You don't have to do the work, but you must acknowledge, route to the right owner, and close the loop
- **Ownership is proactive** (claiming responsibility before solutions exist); **accountability is retrospective** (answering for outcomes after). Ownership prevents what accountability later judges
- Director-readiness requires moving from tactical execution to **strategic ownership** — presenting strategy independently, not waiting for direction

## The Signal / Route / Verify Framework

| Step | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **Signal** | Acknowledge fast: *"I see this and will follow up,"* *"On my radar"* — even before you have a plan |
| **Route** | Send to the right owner (you, a report, or a peer team). Don't sit on ambiguous ones |
| **Verify** | Close the loop — confirm the work happened, or that the owner is on it |

You don't have to *do* the work. You have to make sure the work doesn't fall through the floor on your watch.

## Actionable Insights

- **Acknowledge fast** with phrases like *"I see this and will follow up"* / *"On my radar"* — even before you have a plan
- **Calibrate response speed to severity**: critical issues need near-immediate ack; minor issues warrant hours, not days
- **Before escalating a non-responsive report**, nudge them first: *"You should respond to this. Better from you than me."*
- **Treat delegation as a loop, not a handoff** — schedule verification touchpoints to confirm progress
- **Regularly self-audit your area** for risks/opportunities and proactively communicate vision rather than waiting for the boss to define it
- **For cross-team ambiguity**, claim the routing process: *"Not sure if this is mine or yours, but we'll figure it out"* — avoids both abdication and empire-building
- **Distinguish coaching from neglect**: intervene when stakeholder concerns escalate, when progress stalls despite reminders, or when reputational risk grows

## Ownership vs Accountability

| | Ownership | Accountability |
|---|---|---|
| **When** | Proactive — before something breaks | Retrospective — after something breaks |
| **Stance** | "I've got this" | "I'll answer for what happened" |
| **Effect** | Prevents the failure | Explains the failure |

> "Accountability only kicks in after something goes wrong. Ownership is how you keep it from going wrong in the first place."

## Delegation vs Abdication

Delegation requires the **Verify** step. Without it, you've just shifted the problem and reset its visibility clock. Schedule the follow-up touchpoint at the moment of delegation, not afterward.

## Strategic vs Tactical Ownership (Director Threshold)

The shift toward director-level work isn't about doing more — it's about:

- Presenting strategy independently, not waiting for direction
- Proactively scanning your domain for risk and opportunity
- Communicating vision rather than executing someone else's

> "When your boss picks up something that's yours, they're not helping you. They're doing your job for you, and everyone around you sees it."
