# Career Bets That Compound

## Key Takeaways

- Careers compound through four kinds of capital — **skill, network, reputation, optionality** — moves that grow several at once are the real bets
- A single well-placed compounding bet (one deep specialization) can offset years of non-compounding mistakes
- Most career "progress" is non-compounding — title bumps, brand-chasing laterals, pure pay raises for identical work — it feels like motion without accumulation
- The **ten-year question** ("which decision would the version of me in ten years prefer?") exposes how trivial short-term salary bumps look against accumulated expertise and relationships
- Reputation only compounds when it's **legible** — concrete, describable wins beat vague titles like "head of special projects"

## The Four Capitals

| Capital | What it is | How it compounds |
|---|---|---|
| **Skill** | Domain depth and judgment | Each problem solved deepens pattern library |
| **Network** | Long-term collaborators (20-50 people over decades) | Trust accrues; introductions become referrals become opportunities |
| **Reputation** | What others can credibly say about you | Each legible win raises the floor of future asks |
| **Optionality** | Doors that stay open | Specialization in a hot area; brand affiliations that carry weight |

**Test every move against all four.** A title-only promotion may bump reputation slightly but does nothing for skill or optionality.

## The Six Compounding Bets

1. **Deep specialization** — aim to be among the top fifty globally in a narrow domain
2. **Early-stage company entry** — broad scope, accelerated learning, equity optionality
3. **Durable 20–50 person networks** — long-term collaborators over decades, not LinkedIn collections
4. **Public visibility** — writing, talks, open work that makes your thinking discoverable
5. **Legible reputation wins** — concrete achievements with describable scope and impact
6. **Peer relationships with slightly-senior colleagues** who grow with you over the long arc

## The Six Non-Compounding Moves

1. Title-only promotions (no scope change)
2. Brand-chasing laterals (FAANG hop for the logo)
3. Pure pay bumps for identical work
4. Decisions made to impress others (family, peers, social media)
5. Generalist drift (career flatness from accumulating diverse roles)
6. Conference-style networking theater (collection vs. collaboration)

## Actionable Insights

- **Evaluate every job move against all four capitals** — not just title or comp
- **Choose specialization depth over title inflation** — be one of the fifty best at something specific, not the 5,000th best at being generic
- **Invest in 20–50 long-term collaborators** over decades rather than collecting LinkedIn contacts at events
- **Make achievements legible** — concrete describable wins beat vague titles
- **Apply the ten-year-self test** before accepting raises, lateral brand-chase moves, or generalist roles
- **Audit your motivations** — are you optimizing for your future self or for an external audience?

## Notable Quotes

> "The careers that compound are the ones where each move sets up the next one, deepens what came before."

> Aim to become "one of the fifty best people in the world" at something specific.
