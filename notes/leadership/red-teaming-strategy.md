# Red-Teaming Your Strategy

## Key Takeaways

- The biggest leadership risk isn't being wrong — it's becoming **confident too early** and stopping verification
- Confidence and correctness are not synonymous; experience can cause leaders to skip the very checks that would catch errors
- Red-teaming cannot rely on cultural preference or brave dissenters — it must be **designed into the decision process** as procedural safeguards
- As timelines compress and stakes rise, optional skepticism vanishes — verification must be mandatory, not optional
- **"Speed doesn't come from skipping checks. It comes from skipping surprises."**

![Southwest Airlines Branson incident — experienced pilots landed at the wrong airport](../images/20260601-2100-red-team-southwest-airlines.png)

In 2014, two experienced Southwest pilots landed at the wrong airport in Branson, Missouri. Visual recognition overrode instrument verification. The failure mode: **unexamined confidence under time pressure** — the exact failure mode that hurts leaders making strategic decisions.

## Actionable Insights

- **Assign a designated falsifier** on critical decisions — someone whose job is to disprove, not defend, assumptions
- **Define explicit kill criteria** before launching any initiative — "what would make us stop?"
- **Maintain an assumption ledger** — each strategic bet logged with confidence level and validation date
- **Separate the advocate from the validator** — the person proposing the decision must not be the one verifying it
- **Slow down verification rigor — don't speed it up** — as you near irreversible decisions
- **Time-box pre-mortems to 15–20 minutes** to force discipline (avoid endless debate)
- **Document differentiating signals** between similar options before committing (Two-Runway Test)

## The Seven Red-Teaming Mechanisms

| # | Mechanism | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Disconfirming Evidence Check (DEC)** | Assign someone to actively falsify, not defend, assumptions |
| 2 | **Two-Runway Test** | Document signals that differentiate similar options before commitment |
| 3 | **Time-Boxed Pre-Mortem** | 15–20 min structured failure analysis |
| 4 | **Kill Criteria Up Front** | Define explicit stopping conditions before launch |
| 5 | **Independent Verification** | Separate advocate from validator on critical assumptions |
| 6 | **Assumption Ledger** | Track each assumption, confidence level, and validation date |
| 7 | **Slow-Down-at-the-Brink Rule** | Increase verification rigor as decisions become irreversible |

## Why Procedural, Not Cultural

A culture that "values dissent" still fails when the loudest voice or the senior person decides. Red-teaming as procedure means:

- The falsifier exists *by role*, not by personality
- Kill criteria are written *before* the team is invested in the outcome
- Verification happens *because the process requires it*, not because someone was brave enough to raise a hand

> "The real risk in leadership isn't being wrong, it's becoming confident too early."
