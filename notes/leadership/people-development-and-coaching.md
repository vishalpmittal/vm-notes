# People Development and Coaching

## Key Takeaways

- When similar complaints arise repeatedly across different people, the root cause is typically systemic, not personal — fix the system before addressing the individual
- When someone brings you a problem, ask "what would you do?" first — half the time their instinct is right and they just needed permission
- Reframe nervousness as a positive signal — it means you're doing something meaningful; most people rarely feel nervous because they avoid stretching themselves
- Treat feedback like stand-up comedy: polish comes from relentless testing and iteration, not from getting it right the first time
- Key test: if the same complaints appear with different people, it's structural; if the structure changed tomorrow, would this person adapt? Most would

## Diagnosing Difficult Behavior

**Before your next difficult conversation about an engineer, check three things:**

**1. Is ownership actually clear?**

- Symptoms: engineers step into unclear domains, reopen decisions, sound territorial
- Fix: document who owns the service, who makes final calls, who handles incidents, who owns planning

**2. Are decisions documented and closed?**

- Symptoms: engineers reopen discussions, escalate, challenge settled decisions
- Root cause: decisions get reversed by whoever pushes hardest, making escalation rational
- Fix: record what was decided, who decided, who was consulted, when it can be revisited
- Reinforce "disagree and commit" — discussion is open pre-decision, teams move forward together afterward

**3. Is the system rewarding this behavior?**

- Symptoms: someone everyone complains about persists despite conversations
- Root cause: they're fast, know critical systems, or receive praise from leadership regardless of behavior
- Fix: examine what actually gets rewarded, tolerated, or called out
- Netflix culture memo: "company values are revealed by who gets rewarded, promoted, or tolerated"

**When it really is the person:** Ask "If the structure changed tomorrow, would this person adapt?" Most would. Those who won't signal a genuine individual issue — but fix the system first.

## Feedback Mastery

- **Receiving feedback:** don't defend — say "I hear you," then reflect later with distance
- **Asking for feedback:** be specific ("did my answer sound defensive?") or use numerical scoring to force concrete suggestions; vague asks get vague answers
- **Feedback beyond work:** ask trusted friends targeted, permission-giving questions about health, relationships, personal growth
- **AI as feedback tool:** explicitly ask for critical analysis (e.g., "be harsh") to counteract LLMs' default agreeableness

## Coaching Approach

- When someone brings a problem, ask "what would you do?" before offering solutions — builds judgment and reveals their thinking
- Reframe nervousness as signal: it means the work matters and you're growing
- Most "high-stakes" situations aren't — everyone forgets about it a day later
