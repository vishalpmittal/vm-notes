# Breaking Procrastination

## Key Takeaways

- Procrastination isn't about the task — it's about being stuck in the wrong **mode** (lazy-self vs productive-self); the whole fight is supplying enough **activation energy** to switch
- Don't start with the hardest thing — start with **flow tasks** (small, easy, low-stakes work) to build momentum, then ride the wave into the hard work
- Mind and body are coupled — when stuck mentally, change your physical state first (brisk walk, pushups); a Stanford study found walking lifted creative output ~60%
- For blank-page paralysis, **let AI go first** — voice-dump your thinking into ChatGPT/Claude for a rough first pass; editing is a lower gear than creating
- Common thread across all four tactics: lower the activation energy required to start — almost never solve procrastination by trying harder

## Actionable Insights

### When you can't start a hard task

Don't try to muscle into it. Run two or three **flow tasks** first — explicitly tiny, easy things (reply to an invoice, fix a small bug, outline a draft, clear an inbox folder). The point isn't the output of the flow task; it's crossing from lazy-mode to productive-mode. After 3-4, you'll find yourself able to start the hard thing.

### When you're sunk into the couch / mentally flat

Don't try to think your way out. **Change your physical state** for 5-15 minutes — brisk walk outside, treadmill, pushups, anything that raises heart rate. Don't expect motivation; expect a different baseline state when you sit back down. The mental "I can't focus" usually evaporates after the body wakes up.

### When you can't start writing / can't face a blank page

Don't try to produce from nothing. **Voice-dump everything in your head** at an LLM (ChatGPT/Claude voice mode), then ask for a rough first pass. What it returns is rarely usable as-is — that's fine, because now you're **reacting and editing** instead of **creating**, which is a fundamentally lower gear. The cost of starting drops from "make something out of nothing" to "talk for two minutes."

### The mental model

You're not one person — you're two:
- **Productive-self** — energy, ideas, starting feels easy
- **Lazy-self** — starting feels impossible

Which shows up depends on sleep, weather, food, mood — not willpower. So procrastination diagnostics shouldn't be "why am I lazy?" but "what's the smallest move I can make to flip the mode?" Activation energy is the variable to lower; willpower is the wrong lever.
