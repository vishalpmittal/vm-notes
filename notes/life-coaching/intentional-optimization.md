# Intentional Optimization

## Key Takeaways

- Naval Ravikant: "The only real test of intelligence is if you get what you want out of life" — by that measure, many high-achievers are failing
- Most people optimize for goals they absorbed from parents, peers, and culture — never stopping to check if those goals are actually theirs
- Success has a scoreboard (money, titles); fulfillment doesn't — so we default to optimizing what we can measure
- Small dial adjustments compound: a few dials moved a few points each can change how your whole life feels without changing your job, salary, or address

## Actionable Insights

**The life dials — audit where each one is set:**

- Money
- Autonomy over your time
- Learning and growth in things you care about
- Creative or meaningful work (even on the side)
- Quality of people around you
- Health and energy
- Time for people who matter

Most people have one dial at 10 and every other at 2. The smart move is deciding your mix on purpose.

**Adjust, don't reinvent — pick one dial this month:**

- Want more creative work? Incorporate it into your current role or start something small outside it
- Want more learning? Carve out a few hours a week
- Something draining you? Identify exactly what it is (usually 1-2 things, not everything) — delegate it, automate it, or have the conversation you've been avoiding
- Schedule it — the default is always to keep doing what you're already doing

**Three self-checks:**

1. **Interesting people** — Am I around people who challenge me and make me think differently? (Podcasts and books count)
2. **Learning velocity** — Am I getting better at things I genuinely care about? Not professional development for the sake of it
3. **Energy** — Am I energized or drained by how I spend my time? Consistently drained = pointed in the wrong direction
