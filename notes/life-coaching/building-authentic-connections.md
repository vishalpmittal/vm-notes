# Building Authentic Connections

## Key Takeaways

- The problem isn't who you know — it's what you do after meeting them; most connections die from neglect, not absence
- Networking is a deposit/withdrawal system — people who only show up to ask favors train others to dread their name; people who make deposits (congrats, articles, intros) get instant yes's
- The first deposit is a specific follow-up within 48 hours — reference something concrete they said, add value, include a next step
- Staying remembered over 12 months requires being a good friend to people you barely know — note what they care about, follow up on their milestones
- Introducing two people who should know each other is the highest-value deposit — you become the hub

## Actionable Insights

**The 48-hour follow-up:**

- Reference something specific (the story they told, the problem they mentioned)
- Add value (an article, a resource, an intro)
- Include a clear next step (coffee, a call)
- "Nice to meet you" signals autopilot — specificity puts you in the top 5% of people they met that week

**Staying alive over 12 months:**

- Write down what people are into when you meet them (notes app, whatever) — most people forget within a month
- Follow up on things they mentioned: stressed about a launch? Ask how it went in 2 weeks. Got promoted? Quick congrats
- Forward interesting articles — "idk if relevant, but thought I'd share"; no response expected, but keeps the connection warm
- Use AI to find relevant articles in someone's domain if you're not a heavy reader — 30 seconds for something worth sending

**Connect the dots:**

- Introduce people who should know each other: "Kim, meet Steve. You're both working on [thing]"
- Don't be protective about your network — both sides remember you for it
